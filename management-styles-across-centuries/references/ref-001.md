# ref-001 — Chandler, The Visible Hand

**Source:** Alfred D. Chandler Jr., *The Visible Hand: The Managerial Revolution in American Business* (1977)  
**Date accessed:** —

## Key content

[Placeholder. Chandler's central thesis: the modern business enterprise replaced market mechanisms with administrative coordination when the volume of economic activity reached a level that made internalisation more efficient than market transactions. The "visible hand" of management superseded Adam Smith's "invisible hand" — but only when transport and communication infrastructure made large-scale coordination feasible.]

## Relevance

Foundational text for the infrastructure-co-evolution argument in the essay. Directly supports the Phase 2 (wire and rail) section. Also relevant to CL-2026-012 as context for understanding why lean production emerged when it did.

## Quality note

Pulitzer Prize–winning scholarly work. Widely cited but also critiqued for American-centrism and for underweighting labour relations. The infrastructure argument is strong; the implicit assumption that managerial capitalism was inevitable is more contested.
