# CL-2026-011 — Maillard reactions dominate espresso flavour at medium roast

**Selection mode:** SWEEP  
**Domain:** Coffee chemistry, roasting science  
**Scope:** Medium-roast single-origin beans, first crack to onset of second crack

---

## Claim

In medium-roast coffee, Maillard reaction products (melanoidins, pyrazines, furanones) are the primary contributors to perceived flavour complexity, outweighing caramelisation and Strecker degradation products.

## Predictions

If true: (1) chemical analysis of medium-roast beans should show higher concentrations of Maillard-specific compounds than caramelisation markers; (2) sensory panels should correlate perceived complexity with Maillard marker levels rather than sugar-browning indicators; (3) the dominance should diminish at darker roast levels where pyrolysis overtakes Maillard chemistry.

## Constraints

- Maillard, caramelisation, and Strecker pathways overlap in temperature range and share some precursors — clean separation is methodologically difficult.
- Sensory perception is subjective; panel calibration matters.
- Green bean composition (sugar content, amino acid profile) varies by origin and processing method.
