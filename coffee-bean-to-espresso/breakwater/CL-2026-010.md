# CL-2026-010 — Stirring espresso improves extraction uniformity

**Selection mode:** REQUEST  
**Domain:** Extraction physics, specialty coffee  
**Scope:** Espresso preparation (pressurised percolation, 6–10 bar, 18–22 g dose)

---

## Claim

Stirring or mechanically redistributing the coffee bed before or during extraction produces a more uniform flow through the puck, reducing channelling and increasing extraction yield consistency.

## Predictions

If true: (1) measured extraction yields (refractometer TDS) should show lower variance across repeated shots when a stirring/WDT step is applied versus a control without it; (2) flow-rate profiles during extraction should be smoother (fewer spikes indicating channel formation); (3) the effect should be reproducible across different grind settings and dose weights within the stated scope.

## Constraints

- Fluid dynamics of porous media flow (Darcy's law) governs baseline expectations.
- Particle size distribution from the grinder is an upstream variable that stirring cannot change — it can only affect spatial arrangement.
- Roast age (degassing) affects puck resistance independently of distribution.
