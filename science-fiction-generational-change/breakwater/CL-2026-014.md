# CL-2026-014 — Star Trek's communicator inspired the mobile phone

**Selection mode:** REQUEST  
**Domain:** History of technology, science fiction influence  
**Scope:** Martin Cooper / Motorola DynaTAC development, 1968–1983

---

## Claim

Martin Cooper's development of the first handheld mobile phone (Motorola DynaTAC, 1973 prototype) was directly inspired by the communicator device depicted in the original Star Trek television series (1966–1969).

## Predictions

If true: (1) Cooper or his team should have cited Star Trek as an influence in contemporaneous records, not only in retrospective interviews; (2) the design brief or early prototypes should show formal similarities to the communicator that go beyond the generic concept of a portable radio; (3) alternative explanations for the DynaTAC's form factor (miniaturisation trends in radio technology, military walkie-talkie heritage) should be insufficient to explain the specific design choices.

## Constraints

- Retrospective attribution is unreliable: successful inventors are incentivised to attach compelling origin stories to their work.
- The concept of portable two-way radio predates Star Trek (military SCR-300, 1940s; walkie-talkies were consumer products by the 1960s).
- Cooper has explicitly credited Star Trek in interviews, but interview statements decades later are a weak form of evidence for causal influence.
- Inspiration is not causation — even if Star Trek shaped Cooper's imagination, the enabling technology (cellular network architecture, battery miniaturisation) was independently developed.
